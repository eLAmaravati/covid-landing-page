Overview

What is Coronavirus?

Coronaviruses are a type of virus. There are many different kinds, and some cause disease. A newly identified type has caused a recent outbreak of respiratory illness, now called COVID-19. Lauren Sauer, M.S., the director of operations with the Johns Hopkins Office of Critical Event Preparedness and Response, explains the situation.

Contagion

How the Virus Spreads

COVID-19 spreads through respiratory droplets, direct contact, and contaminated surfaces. Airborne transmission in enclosed spaces increases the risk of infection.

Air Transmission

Virus spreads through airborne particles, especially in crowded or enclosed spaces.

Human Contacts

Direct physical contact, like handshakes or hugs, can spread the virus.

Containted Objects

Touching infected surfaces and then touching your face can spread the virus.

Prevention

What Should We Do

Protect yourself and others by following essential preventive measures. Stay informed, practice good hygiene, and make responsible choices to reduce the risk of infection.

01

Wear Masks

Masks help prevent the spread of germs, especially in crowded or indoor spaces. Wearing a mask reduces the risk of transmission, protecting both yourself and those around you.

02

Wash Your Hands

Regularly washing your hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds helps eliminate harmful germs and bacteria. If soap and water aren't available, use hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol.

03

Use Nose - Rag

Always cover your nose and mouth with a clean tissue or cloth when sneezing or coughing. Dispose of the tissue immediately, and wash your hands afterward to prevent the spread of germs.

04

Avoid Contacts

Maintain a safe distance from others, especially in crowded places. Avoid physical contact such as handshakes and hugs to reduce the risk of transmitting or contracting illnesses.
